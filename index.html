BOILED EGGS
‚Äî I N G R E D I E N T S ‚Äî
  • eggs
  • ice-cold water

1  Place eggs in a single layer on the bottom of a pot.
2  Cover with cold water so the water level is about an inch higher than the eggs.
3  Place pot over high heat and bring water to a rolling boil.
4  Choose your method based on desired firmness:
  • For Soft Boiled (Simmer Method): Once boiling, reduce heat to a simmer. Cook for 3-4 minutes (runny yolk), 5-6 minutes (firm white/runny yolk), or 7-8 minutes (jammy yolk).
  • For Hard Boiled (Steep Method): Once boiling, remove pot from heat and cover with a lid. Let stand for 9-11 minutes (9 for medium, 10 for large, 11 for extra-large).
5  Drain the hot water and immediately transfer eggs to an ice bath (or run under cold water) until cooled.
6  Serving/Storage: Soft boiled eggs are best served immediately. Hard boiled eggs (shell on) can be refrigerated for up to 1 week.

Serves 4 to 6.

It's teeming with all of the restorative properties of ginger and turmeric and thickened with white rice, which swells as it cooks and turns a watery broth into a hearty, comforting porridge. This is exactly what you need when what you need is to get well. Rice acts as a thickening agent when cooked this way, thanks to its naturally occurring starch, so be sure to simmer the soup until the grains of rice are no longer distinct from the broth they cook in‚Äîand the whole thing turns rich and cozy.

2  Start the soup:
  • Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the scallions (white and light green parts), garlic, ginger, and 1 1/4 teaspoons turmeric. Cook, stirring often, until the aromatics are softened and fragrant but not browned, 3 to 4 minutes.

4  Season the soup and serve:
  • Squeeze the juice of 2 limes into the soup.
  • Stir in 1 teaspoon black pepper. Taste the soup and add more salt if you think it needs it.
  • Divide the soup among soup bowls. Garnish it with the reserved sliced scallion greens, some cilantro leaves, a good crank of black pepper, and an additional drizzle of olive oil.
