 🍳 EGGS 🍳

DEVILED EGGS
TIP: Older eggs peel easier than fresh ones. Try to buy your eggs a week in advance.

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 12 eggs
  • 3 tsp dijon mustard
  • ⅓ cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp minced shallot / onion
  • 1 tsp sugar, ⅛ tsp salt, ⅛ tsp pepper
  • ¼ tsp tabasco sauce
  • Paprika, chives, pickle juice (for garnish)

1  Place eggs in a large saucepan and cover with water. Transfer to stovetop over high heat until water begins to boil.
2  Boil for one minute, cover with lid, and remove from heat. Allow to sit for 17 minutes and then drain and transfer to an ice bath.
3  Peel eggs and set aside. Slice each egg in half lengthwise and gently remove yolk halves into small mixing bowl. Arrange egg whites on serving platter.
4  Use a fork to mash the yolks together with mustard, mayonnaise, butter, lemon juice, shallot/onion, sugar, salt, pepper, and tabasco.
5  Add mayonnaise as necessary to get desired creamy consistency, then piping bag or spoon mixture back into egg halves.
6  Sprinkle with paprika and chives and serve.

BOILED EGGS
Soft boiled eggs are best served immediately, but hard boiled eggs with the shell on can be refrigerated for up to a week.

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • eggs
  • ice-cold water

1  Place eggs in a single layer on the bottom of a pot. Cover with cold water (water level 1 inch higher than eggs).
2  Place pot over high heat and bring water to a rolling boil.
3  Soft Boiled: Reduce heat to simmer. Cook 3-4 mins (runny), 5-6 mins (firm white), or 7-8 mins (jammy).
4  Hard Boiled: Remove from heat and cover. Let stand 9-11 minutes depending on size.
5  Drain hot water and transfer to ice bath until cooled.

BREAKFAST BURRITO
NOTE: Warm the tortillas slightly in the microwave or on a skillet to make them pliable before rolling.

  • chili powder
  • paprika
  • onion powder

 🍞 BREAD 🍞

CRUSTY NO-KNEAD
If you don't have a Dutch Oven, you'll lose the trapped steam and the delicious crust. Still edible!

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 2 tsp active dry yeast
  • 2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1½ cups very warm tap water
  • 3 cups bread flour

1  Whisk yeast and salt in a large bowl with warm water.
2  Add in all the flour at once and stir until a sticky dough forms.
3  Cover with a kitchen towel and leave for 2-3 hours to rise until doubled in size.
4  Preheat oven to 450°F with Dutch oven inside and lid on.
5  Dust dough with flour and form it into a ball on parchment paper.
6  Lift paper and dough into Dutch oven and return to oven.
7  Bake 30 minutes covered, then 12 minutes uncovered.
8  Remove and place on cooling rack for at least 10 minutes before slicing.


PIZZA DOUGH
NOTE: If the yeast mixture doesn't foam after 5 minutes, your yeast may be dead. Start over with fresh yeast.

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 1 cup water, warmed
  • 1 tsp instant yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt

1  Combine water, yeast, and sugar in medium bowl. Let stand until foamy (5 mins).
2  Combine flour and salt. Mix foamy yeast water into flour.
3  Knead on floured surface, then place in lightly oiled bowl and cover with cloth.
4  Let rise 30 minutes, then cut in half. Bag or use immediately.



 🥔 POTATO 🥔

ROAST POTATOES
SECRET: The baking soda breaks down the potato edges, and shaking them roughly creates a mash-like paste that crisps up perfectly.

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 4 lbs Yukon Gold or Russet potatoes, peeled/chunked
  • ½ tsp baking soda (for water)
  • 5 tbsp olive oil or animal fat
  • Rosemary, garlic, pepper, parsley

1  Preheat oven to 450°F. Boil water with salt and baking soda.
2  Add potatoes, simmer 10 mins until easily pierced. Drain and let moisture evaporate.
3  Cook oil/fat with rosemary, garlic, and pepper until garlic is golden. Strain oil, keep solids.
4  Toss potatoes with infused oil, salt, and pepper. Shake roughly to create starchy paste.
5  Roast 20 mins. Flip. Roast 30-35 mins more until crisp. Toss with garlic solids and parsley.


POTATO SALAD
TIP: Allow potatoes to cool slightly before adding mayonnaise to prevent it from splitting and becoming oily.

 I N G R E D I E N T S

  • 2 green onions, chopped, fresh dill

1  Boil potatoes in salted water until fork-tender (10-12 minutes).
2  Drain potatoes and return to pot (off heat).
3  Add mustard, mayonnaise, peas/beans, onions, and dill. Season with pepper.
4  Stir to coat and serve warm or chilled.


POTATO DAUPHINOISE
TIP: Slice potatoes about ¼-inch thick. If they are too thin they will disintegrate; too thick and they won't absorb the cream.

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2+ cups Gruyère cheese, grated
  • 2½ lbs Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups warmed heavy cream
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves

1  Preheat oven to 350°F. Butter a 4-quart dish.
2  Simmer sliced potatoes in pot with cream, milk, garlic, and spices for 5-7 mins. Remove bay leaves.
3  Layer half potatoes in dish, top with half cheese. Repeat with remaining potatoes and cream mixture.
4  Top with remaining cheese. Bake 45 mins until golden. Rest 15 mins.

 🥘 SOUP 🥘

ROASTED TOMATO
Substitute coconut milk to make it vegan!

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • dozen tomatoes, halved
  • large knob ginger
  • 2 heads garlic, unpeeled
  • red onion
  • bell pepper
  • carrot
  • 3 cups vegetable stock
  • balsamic vinegar
  • heavy cream
  • fresh basil

1  Preheat oven to 350°F.
2  Place halved tomatoes, onion, bell pepper, garlic, and carrot on a baking sheet.
3  Drizzle everything generously with olive oil and sprinkle with salt.
4  Bake for 35-40 minutes until roasted and slightly charred.
5  Squeeze the roasted garlic cloves out of their skins and discard the skins.
6  Scrape the pan to collect all juices and flavor bits, then transfer all roasted vegetables to a pot or blending container.
7  Add a splash of balsamic vinegar and fresh basil.
8  Stir in a splash of heavy cream, and season to taste.


BROCCOLI CHEDDAR
NOTE: Grate your own cheese block. Pre-shredded cheese contains anti-caking agents that can make the soup grainy.

 I N G R E D I E N T S

  • ¼ cup flour, 2 cups chicken stock, 2 cups cream
  • 3 cups broccoli, 2 carrots
  • 8 oz sharp cheddar, grated
  • Spices: salt, pepper, paprika, dry mustard, cayenne

1  Sauté onion and garlic in 1 tbsp butter. Set aside.
2  Make roux: melt 4 tbsp butter, whisk in flour. Cook 3-4 mins.
3  Whisk in stock and cream. Simmer 15 mins to thicken.
4  Add veggies, onion mixture, and spices. Simmer 20-25 mins.
5  Remove from heat. Stir in cheese until melted.

 🐄 BEEF 🐄

EYE OF ROUND ROAST
TIP: Eye of Round is very lean! To keep it tender, you must slice it very thinly against the grain before serving.

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 1 kg Beef Eye of Round Roast
  • 4-5 medium potatoes (Yukon or Red)
  • 4-5 carrots, peeled
  • 2 onions
  • 2-3 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 3 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste (optional)
  • Thyme, rosemary, salt, pepper, oil

1  Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C). Pat beef very dry and season generously with salt and pepper. Cut potatoes and carrots into large chunks and onions into wedges.
2  Heat oil in Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear beef until dark brown crust forms on all sides (3-4 mins per side). Remove beef and set aside.
3  Lower heat to medium. Sauté onions (and garlic/tomato paste) for 2 mins. Pour in wine or splash of broth to deglaze, scraping up browned bits.
4  Return beef to pot. Add broth until it reaches halfway up the meat (do not submerge). Add herbs. Cover and bake for 1 hour.
5  Remove pot, arrange carrots and potatoes around the meat in the liquid. Cover and bake another 1 to 1.5 hours until meat is fork-tender.
6  Transfer meat to board and tent with foil. Rest for 20 minutes (crucial for moisture). Slice thin and serve with veggies and pan juices.
