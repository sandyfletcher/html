 🍳 EGGS 🍳

STEAM-BOILED
If your eggs are refrigerated, add an extra minute to the steaming time.

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 6 eggs

1  Add about an inch of water to a pot and place an expanding steamer ring inside.
2  Place the pot over high heat, cover, and bring the water to a rolling boil.
3  Carefully place the eggs into the steamer basket - or directly into the water if you don't have one - then cover and reduce heat to medium-high.
4  Cook for 6½ mins for a runny yolk, 8 mins for jammy yolk, or 12 mins for firm.
5  Immediately transfer eggs to an ice bath for 5 minutes to stop the cooking process, then peel and serve.



 🍞 BREAD 🍞

CRUSTY NO-KNEAD
If you don't have a Dutch Oven, you'll lose the trapped steam and the delicious crust. Still edible!

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 2 tsp active dry yeast
  • 2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1½ cups very warm tap water
  • 3 cups bread flour

1  Whisk yeast and salt in a large bowl with warm water.
2  Add in all the flour at once and stir until a sticky dough forms.
3  Cover with a kitchen towel and leave for 2-3 hours to rise until doubled in size.
4  Preheat oven to 450°F with Dutch oven inside and lid on.
5  Dust dough with flour and form it into a ball on parchment paper.
6  Lift paper and dough into Dutch oven and return to oven.
7  Bake 30 minutes covered, then 12 minutes uncovered.
8  Remove and place on cooling rack for at least 10 minutes before slicing.



 🥔 POTATO 🥔

POTATO SALAD
TIP: Allow potatoes to cool slightly before adding mayonnaise to prevent it from splitting and becoming oily.

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 1½ cups yellow potatoes, cubed
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard, 2 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 cup chopped snap peas or green beans
  • 2 green onions, chopped, fresh dill

1  Boil potatoes in salted water until fork-tender (10-12 minutes).
2  Drain potatoes and return to pot (off heat).
3  Add mustard, mayonnaise, peas/beans, onions, and dill. Season with pepper.
4  Stir to coat and serve warm or chilled.



 🥘 SOUP 🥘

ROASTED TOMATO
Substitute coconut milk to make it vegan!

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • dozen tomatoes, halved
  • large knob ginger
  • 2 heads garlic, unpeeled
  • red onion
  • bell pepper
  • carrot
  • 3 cups vegetable stock
  • balsamic vinegar
  • heavy cream
  • fresh basil

1  Preheat oven to 350°F.
2  Place halved tomatoes, onion, bell pepper, garlic, and carrot on a baking sheet.
3  Drizzle everything generously with olive oil and sprinkle with salt.
4  Bake for 35-40 minutes until roasted and slightly charred.
5  Squeeze the roasted garlic cloves out of their skins and discard the skins.
6  Scrape the pan to collect all juices and flavor bits, then transfer all roasted vegetables to a pot or blending container.
7  Add a splash of balsamic vinegar and fresh basil.
8  Stir in a splash of heavy cream, and season to taste.



 🥗 SALAD 🥗

NIÇOISE
The French are very specific about names — for maximum authenticity scatter half a cup of niçoise olives over the plates at Step 5.

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 10 oz green beans, trimmed
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 lb red potatoes, diced large
  • 2 tbsp dry white wine
  • ¼ cup white wine vinegar
  • 2 tbsp shallot, minced
  • 2 tbsp dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • freshly-ground black pepper, to taste
  • ¾ cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 8 cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 head Boston lettuce, separated into leaves
  • 6 radishes, quartered
  • 2 5½ oz cans tuna in oil, drained

1  Prepare stovetop with 3 pots:
  • Small:  Salt water, bring to a boil, then add green beans and cook for 4 minutes.
  • Medium: Follow the steam-boiled egg method from above.
  • Large:  Cover potatoes with cold water, season with salt, and bring to a simmer. Cook until fork-tender (~5 minutes), drain, and transfer to a medium bowl to drizzle with wine.
2  Meanwhile, create an ice bath big enough to cool the beans followed by the eggs. Pat dry and let ingredients sit once cooled.
3  Whisk the vinegar, shallot, mustard, thyme, salt, and pepper together in a bowl, then whisk in the olive oil slowly to emulsify.
4  Dress the potatoes with a quarter of the prepared dressing while still warm to help them absorb the flavors of the vinaigrette.
5  Quarter the cooked eggs and toss the tomatoes in a small bowl with salt and pepper to taste, pouring any excess juices into the dressing.
6  Plate by starting with the lettuce, arrange potatoes, beans, radishes, eggs, tomatoes, and tuna on top, and drizzle with remaining dressing.



 🥡 SIDES 🥡

PINEAPPLE GUACAMOLE
FLAVOR TIP: Don't skip the grilling step! Caramelized pineapple adds depth that raw fruit lacks.

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 4 pineapple discs, 4 avocados
  • Glaze: lime, brown sugar, honey, chili powder
  • red onion, tomato, jalapeño, cilantro, cumin

1  Brush pineapple with glaze. Grill 3-4 mins per side. Dice.
2  Mash avocados. Mix with lime, onion, tomato, spices.
3  Fold in pineapple. Serve with chips.



 🍝 PASTA 🍝

BAKED FETA
NOTE: Use a block of feta, not crumbles. Crumbles contain anti-caking agents and won't melt into a sauce.

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 2 pints cherry tomatoes, 8 oz feta block
  • 9 oz pasta, olive oil, garlic, basil

1  Bake tomatoes, oil, and feta at 400°F for 30 mins, then 450°F for 10 mins.
2  Cook pasta, reserve water.
3  Stir garlic into hot feta/tomatoes to create sauce.
4  Toss in pasta and basil.

 🐖 PORK 🐖

PEPPER PULLED PORK
Any dark caramel soda will do the job and provide a slightly different taste.

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 2½-3 lb pork butt/shoulder
  • 750ml Dr. Pepper
  • 1 medium onion, cut into wedges
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1½ tsp dry ground mustard
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • ¼ cup apple cider vinegar
  • 3 tbsp worcestershire sauce
  • barbecue sauce
  • bread rolls or buns

1  Distribute onion in bottom of slow cooker, then place pork on top.
2  Add garlic, mustard, cayenne, salt, pepper, apple cider vinegar, and worcestershire sauce, then pour soda overtop.
3  Cook on high for 4-5 hours, or low for 8.
4  Carefully remove pork and place on cutting board, then shred.
5  Return shredded pork to slow cooker for another hour.
6  Drain remaining juices, then add barbecue sauce to meat and onion mixture to reach desired sauciness.
7  Portion onto toasted buns and serve — pairs well with coleslaw or oven fries!


 🐄 BEEF 🐄

BRAISED POT ROAST
CRITICAL STEP: Sear the roast thoroughly until dark brown. This creates the flavor base for the sauce.

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 2-3 lb chuck roast, onion, garlic
  • red wine, beef broth, thyme, rosemary
  • potatoes, carrots

1  Sear seasoned roast in Dutch oven. Remove.
2  Cook onions/garlic. Deglaze with wine. Add broth/herbs.
3  Return meat. Braise 300°F 1.5 hours.
4  Add veggies. Braise 1 hour more.

 🎂 DESSERT 🎂

BANANA BREAD
Frozen bananas create extra liquid, but you can microwave fresh ones to artificially ripen them.

 I N G R E D I E N T S 
  • 1¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 6 ripe bananas, peeled
  • 8 tbsp unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 2 large eggs
  • ¾ cup packed light brown sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tsp granulated sugar

1  Adjust oven rack to middle position, preheat oven to 350°F, and grease a 9x5" loaf pan.
2  In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt.
3  Place bananas in a strainer set over a bowl and let it drain, stirring occasionally, for 15 minutes.
4  Transfer collected liquid to a medium saucepan and cook over medium-high heat until it reduces to a ¼ cup — about 5 minutes.
5  Return bananas to bowl, mash in reduced liquid until mostly smooth, then whisk in melted butter, eggs, brown sugar, and vanilla.
6  Pour banana mixture into flour mixture, stir until just combined, then transfer batter into prepared pan.
7  Sprinkle granulated sugar evenly over loaf, then bake until skewer inserted in center of load comes out clean — about 55 minutes to 1¼ hour, rotating halfway through baking.
8  Let loaf cool in pan for 10 minutes, then remove and let cool on wire rack for 1 hour before serving.
