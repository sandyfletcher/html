Recipe Overview

Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 30-35 minutes (bake) + 2 mins (broil)
Total Time: Approximately 55-60 minutes
Servings: 8-10 servings
Difficulty: Easy to Medium

Ingredients

  • 1 pound cavatappi pasta, cooked according to package directions
  • 16 ounces mozzarella cheese, grated
  • 16 ounces Colby Jack cheese, grated
  • 8 ounces Cheddar cheese, (sharp or medium) grated
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper (freshly ground preferred)
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 (12-ounce) can evaporated milk
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Cook cavatappi according to package instructions until al dente. Drain well and set aside. (Do not overcook, as it will bake further.)
  3. In a large bowl, combine all three grated cheeses (mozzarella, Colby Jack, Cheddar). Divide this mixture roughly in half, placing one half in another bowl. Set both bowls aside.
  4. In a small bowl, combine the garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Divide this seasoning mixture in half and set aside.
  5. Melt butter in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat. Once melted, stir in half of the seasoning mixture and the flour. Cook, stirring constantly, until the mixture becomes paste-like and lightly golden, about 3 to 5 minutes. This is your roux.
  6. Gradually whisk in the evaporated milk until the mixture is smooth and begins to thicken.
  7. Slowly pour in the heavy cream and add the remaining half of the seasoning mixture. Continue to whisk and cook until the sauce is well combined and starts to simmer gently.
  8. Whisk in the Dijon mustard until fully incorporated and the sauce thickens more.
  9. Reduce heat to low. Slowly add one half of the total cheese mixture (one of your prepared bowls) to the sauce, a handful at a time, stirring continuously until each addition is melted and smooth before adding more. Do not boil.
  10. Once all of that first half of cheese is melted into a smooth sauce, remove from heat. Gently stir in the cooked cavatappi pasta until all noodles are thoroughly coated with the cheese sauce.
  11. Spread half of the sauced macaroni into the prepared 9x13-inch baking dish. Sprinkle evenly with half of the remaining shredded cheese (from your second bowl of cheese).
  12. Top with the rest of the sauced macaroni. Sprinkle the very top layer evenly with all the remaining shredded cheese.
  13. Bake in the preheated oven for 25 to 30 minutes, or until the cheese is melted, bubbly, and the edges are golden.
  14. For a crispier, golden-brown top (optional): Turn on the oven broiler. Broil for 1 to 2 minutes, watching very carefully to prevent burning. The cheese should be beautifully browned.
  15. Remove from oven and let it rest for 5-10 minutes before serving. This helps the sauce set. Enjoy your amazing homemade Mac and Cheese!

Chef's Tips & Notes

"For an extra layer of flavor, consider adding a pinch of cayenne pepper or a dash of hot sauce to your cheese sauce. If you like a bit of crunch, you can top the mac and cheese with buttered panko breadcrumbs before the final baking."

"Always grate your own cheese! Pre-shredded cheeses contain anti-caking agents that can make your sauce grainy. The extra few minutes of grating are well worth it for a smoother, meltier result."
