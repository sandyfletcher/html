  11. Cover loosely with plastic wrap and allow dough to rise again until an inch above the top of the pan, about 45 minutes.

  13. Bake for about 30 minutes or until golden brown on top - should sound hollow when tapped.
  14. Invert loaves onto wire cooling rack and brush tops with butter.
  15. Allow to cool for 10 minutes before slicing, then store in an airtight container.

Deviled Eggs

  • 3 tsp dijon mustard

  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp minced shallot / onion
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • â…› tsp salt
  • â…› tsp pepper
  • Â¼ tsp tabasco sauce
  • Paprika, chives, pickle juice (for garnish/flavor)
  1. Place eggs in a large saucepan and cover with water.
  2. Transfer to stovetop over high heat until water begins to boil.
  3. Boil for one minute, cover with lid, and remove from heat. Allow to sit for 17 minutes and then drain and transfer to an ice bath.
  4. Peel eggs and set aside.
  5. Slice each egg in half lengthwise and gently remove yolk halves into small mixing bowl. Arrange egg whites on serving platter.
  6. Use a fork to mash the yolks together with mustard, mayonnaise, butter, lemon juice, shallot/onion, sugar, salt, pepper, and tabasco.
  7. Add mayonnaise as necessary to get desired creamy consistency, then piping bag or spoon mixture back into egg halves.
  8. Sprinkle with paprika and chives. Add a dash of pickle juice if desired. Let settle.
